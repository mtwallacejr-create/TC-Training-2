Section 1

Deal Initiation

Every deal opens the same way: LOI to test the deal, Cash PSA to lock it in. Know the difference between the two and when each is in play.

Letter of Intent (LOI) Initiation
What It Is

Non-binding proposal outlining key deal terms — purchase price, carry structure, timeline, and buyer entity — before a formal contract is drafted.

Used For
  • Opening negotiations on creative finance deals
  • Testing seller interest and establishing price/terms
  • Presenting Sub-To, Seller Finance, or Pref-Eq in plain language before committing to a full PSA
Key Steps
  • Fill property address and buyer LLC name
  • Input proposed purchase price and cash-to-seller breakdown
  • State seller carry terms (rate, balloon, payment if any)
  • Set proposed inspection and closing timeline
  • Send to seller; collect signature to confirm intent
Cash PSA Initiation
What It Is

Core Purchase and Sale Agreement for cash, conventional, or investor deals. The binding contract defining every term — purchase price, EMD, timeline, parties, and closing costs.

Used For
  • Standard wholesale assignments where end buyer pays cash or arranges own financing
  • All deal types as the baseline contract — addenda supplement it
  • Establishing the legal framework title uses to process the closing
Key Steps
  • Sections 1.1–1.3: Property address, APN, legal description
  • Sections 1.4–1.5: EMD amount and purchase price breakdown
  • Sections 1.6–1.7: COE date and escrow agent info
  • Sections 1.9–1.12: Inspection period, closing costs, occupancy
  • Signature page: verify all signatures, initials, and dates

Section 3

Assignment & Cancellation

Exiting or unwinding a deal. Two outcomes, two documents — know which situation calls for which.

Assignment Agreement Assignment
What It Is

Transfers the buyer's equitable interest in the PSA to a new end buyer (assignee). Assignee steps into the buyer's position and closes the deal, paying an assignment fee.

Used For
  • Wholesale exit strategy — the primary way wholesalers monetize a deal
  • Collecting the assignment fee from the end buyer
  • Legally documenting the transfer of contract rights
Key Steps
  • Fill assignor and assignee legal names
  • Reference original PSA by property address and effective date
  • Enter assignment fee amount and payment method
  • State that assignee assumes all buyer obligations under the PSA
  • Both parties sign; send copy to title with original PSA
📄 Blank Template
Cancellation Agreement Cancellation
What It Is

Mutual agreement terminating the contract and releasing all parties. Also directs the title company on how to disburse the Earnest Money Deposit.

Used For
  • Deal falls through — buyer cancels or parties agree to unwind
  • Releasing all parties cleanly with no remaining obligations
  • Directing EMD to the correct party
Key Steps
  • Fill property and names of all parties
  • Enter EMD amount and specify release instructions
  • Both parties sign — seller first, then buyer/assignee
  • Send signed cancellation to title company immediately
  • Confirm title company acknowledges and cancels escrow

Section 4

JV & Partnership Structures

When multiple parties bring capital or deal components, a formal JV structure replaces informal handshake deals. Know the two types.

Stacked JV Agreement (Preferred Equity) JV / Partnership
What It Is

JV structured as preferred equity. Investors provide capital and receive a fixed preferred return plus equity participation before profits are split.

Used For
  • Multi-investor deals requiring capital stacking
  • Investors who want preferred return + equity kicker
  • Structuring different return tiers across multiple JV partners
Key Steps
  • Fill all party names (lead investor, co-investors, operating entity)
  • Enter each party's capital contribution and preferred return %
  • Define equity split after the preferred return waterfall
  • Set deal term, exit trigger, and distribution order
  • All members sign; file with operating agreement for deal LLC
Consultant JV Agreement JV / Partnership
What It Is

JV where a consultant, deal finder, or co-wholesaler receives compensation for sourcing or facilitating a deal — structured as a JV rather than a simple referral fee.

Used For
  • Compensating bird dogs, deal finders, or co-wholesalers formally
  • Documenting each party's role and contribution to avoid disputes
  • Any deal where a third party is owed a fee or profit share
Key Steps
  • Define each party's role (lead buyer, consultant, deal sourcer)
  • Enter compensation structure (flat fee, % of assignment fee, or split)
  • Specify payment trigger (at assignment, at closing, etc.)
  • State non-solicitation and confidentiality obligations
  • Both parties sign before any deal docs are sent to seller

Section 5

Creative Finance Loan Agreements

2nd position, preferred equity, and SLA modifications. Know which structure matches which capital arrangement and who holds what security interest.

Subordinated Loan Agreement — 2nd Position Creative Finance
What It Is

Loan agreement where an investor provides bridge capital secured by a second-position lien, subordinate to the existing first mortgage.

Used For
  • Investor-funded "Morby Method" 2nd position deals
  • Providing the buyer capital to fund the gap between 1st mortgage and purchase price
  • Structuring the investor's security interest and repayment terms
Key Steps
  • Fill lender and borrower entity names
  • Enter loan amount (2nd position investment)
  • Set interest rate, monthly payment if any, and maturity date
  • Describe lien position and property as collateral
  • Both parties sign; lender's counsel records 2nd lien at closing
Preferred Equity Loan Agreement Creative Finance
What It Is

Loan structured as preferred equity — investor receives priority preferred return before other equity participates, often with an additional equity percentage.

Used For
  • Pref-Eq structures where investors want priority repayment + equity upside
  • Deals using preferred equity in lieu of a traditional loan or 2nd lien
  • Capital raises where the investor is an equity partner, not just a debt holder
Key Steps
  • Fill investor and borrower/deal entity names
  • Enter preferred return rate (annualized %) and equity participation %
  • Set loan principal, term, and maturity/exit trigger
  • Define default provisions and investor remedies
  • Both parties sign; attach to operating agreement for deal LLC
Addendum to SLA Creative Finance
What It Is

Modification document that amends terms of an existing Subordinated or Preferred Equity Loan Agreement — extensions, rate adjustments, or payoff modifications.

Used For
  • Extending maturity when a deal needs more time to close or refinance
  • Modifying interest rate or repayment structure on an active loan
  • Documenting any agreed change to original SLA terms
Key Steps
  • Reference original SLA by property and execution date
  • Clearly identify which terms are being modified
  • State new terms (new maturity date, rate, payment schedule)
  • Both original parties sign the addendum
  • Attach to original SLA; send copies to all parties and title

Section 6

EMD Lending Documents

Third-party earnest money funding. Four documents govern this process — one to open it, one to evidence the debt, one to pay back the lender, and one to modify it if the timeline shifts.

EMD Lending Documents EMD Lending
What It Is

Set of documents for third-party EMD funding — includes the EMD Loan Agreement plus irrevocable escrow instructions directing the title company on fund application and release.

Used For
  • Deals where the buyer needs a third party to fund the EMD
  • EMD lenders requiring a formal agreement before wiring funds
  • Protecting the lender's interest through irrevocable disbursement instructions
Key Steps
  • Fill EMD lender and borrower (buyer entity) names
  • Enter EMD amount and destination (escrow/title company account)
  • Attach irrevocable escrow instructions directing title on disbursement
  • Specify repayment terms — at closing or on cancellation
  • Send executed docs to title before EMD wire is sent

Addendum to EMD Loan Agreement EMD Lending
What It Is

Modifies the terms of an existing EMD Loan Agreement — most commonly used to extend the repayment deadline when a deal's closing date is pushed back.

Used For
  • When the closing date moves and the EMD loan maturity needs extension
  • Modifying repayment amount or terms on an active EMD loan
  • Documenting any agreed change to the original EMD loan terms
Key Steps
  • Reference original EMD Loan Agreement by date and property
  • State the modification (e.g., new maturity/repayment date)
  • Both original parties (lender + borrower) sign
  • Send copy to title company if escrow instructions are affected
  • Attach to original EMD Lending Documents for the deal file

Section 7

Entity & Authorization Documents

Entity resolutions, credit support, and distressed exits. These documents govern who can sign, who backs the deal, and what happens when it goes sideways.

Credit Sponsorship Agreement Entity / Auth
What It Is

Agreement where a credit sponsor provides their credit to help the buyer qualify for financing. In exchange, the sponsor receives a non-voting membership interest in the buyer's LLC.

Used For
  • Deals where the buyer needs a credit sponsor to qualify for a loan
  • Compensating the sponsor with equity (non-voting LLC membership)
  • Documenting the sponsor's obligations and rights before the deal closes
Key Steps
  • Fill sponsor name and buyer/LLC name
  • Enter the % non-voting membership interest granted to sponsor
  • State sponsor's obligations (guaranty, co-signing, scope)
  • Define when the sponsor's interest terminates (loan payoff, exit, etc.)
  • Both parties sign; attach copy to LLC operating agreement
Single Signer Resolution Entity / Auth
What It Is

Corporate resolution authorizing one designated member of a multi-member LLC to sign all transaction documents on behalf of the entity, without requiring all members to sign each document.

Used For
  • Multi-member LLCs where one person handles the TC/closing process
  • Title companies requiring written authorization before accepting a single signer
  • Any deal where the LLC has multiple members but one person acts as deal lead
Key Steps
  • Fill the full legal name of the LLC
  • List all current members of the LLC
  • Name the designated signer and their title (Manager, Member, etc.)
  • Define scope — specific deal or general authority for all transactions
  • All members sign; send copy to title with Contract Package

Section 8

Addenda & Extensions

Modifications to active deals. When something changes after the contract is signed, one of these two documents keeps the deal alive without canceling everything.

General Addenda (Seller Acknowledgment, etc.) Addenda
What It Is

Supplemental documents that modify, clarify, or add terms to the primary PSA — including seller acknowledgment forms and any deal-specific supplements.

Used For
  • Adding terms to the PSA not covered by the base contract
  • Seller acknowledgment that existing loans remain in seller's name
  • Any amendment or supplement needed before or during the transaction
Key Steps
  • Reference the original PSA by property address and effective date
  • State exactly what is being added, modified, or acknowledged
  • Use unambiguous language — avoid contradicting the base contract
  • All relevant parties sign the addendum
  • Attach to Contract Package; send updated file to title
Extension Addendum to Assignment Addenda
What It Is

Modifies an executed Assignment Agreement to extend the closing deadline, giving the end buyer additional time to close without canceling the transaction.

Used For
  • End buyer needs more time to fund or close — both parties agree
  • Avoiding cancellation when a closing delay is caused by title, lender, or funding
  • Documenting the agreed extension with a new closing date
Key Steps
  • Reference the original Assignment Agreement by property and execution date
  • State the new closing deadline clearly
  • Include any extension fee owed by assignee to assignor
  • Both assignor and assignee sign the addendum
  • Send to title company immediately to update their closing timeline
