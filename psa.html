3. EARNEST MONEY / DEPOSIT.

The EMD shall be deposited with the Escrow Agent within the time specified in Section 1.5. During the Inspection Period, the EMD is fully refundable. After the Inspection Period expires without cancellation, the EMD becomes non-refundable ("hard") except as specifically provided herein. If Seller defaults or is unable to deliver clear title, Buyer shall receive a full refund of the EMD. If Buyer defaults after the Inspection Period expires, the EMD shall be forfeited to Seller as liquidated damages, as Seller's sole and exclusive remedy.

4. INSPECTION PERIOD AND DUE DILIGENCE.

During the Inspection Period defined in Section 1.6, Buyer and Buyer's agents, contractors, and representatives shall have reasonable access to the Property upon 24-hour notice to Seller to conduct any and all inspections, investigations, and tests Buyer deems appropriate, including but not limited to physical inspection, pest inspection, environmental assessment, and title review.

Buyer may cancel this Agreement for any or no reason by delivering written notice to Seller prior to expiration of the Inspection Period, in which case the EMD shall be returned to Buyer in full. Buyer's failure to provide cancellation notice before expiration constitutes Buyer's election to proceed, and the EMD shall go hard (non-refundable) except as provided in Section 3.

5. TITLE, VESTING, AND CLOSING.

Seller shall convey marketable title to the Property to Buyer free and clear of all liens, encumbrances, and assessments except: (a) current year property taxes and assessments not yet due; (b) items shown as exceptions in the Preliminary Title Report acceptable to Buyer; and (c) any easements, CC&Rs, or restrictions of record that do not materially impair Buyer's intended use.

Buyer shall take title vested as: [Vesting], or as directed in writing prior to close of escrow.
